How Calorie Burn is Calculated

We use the MET formula:
Calories = MET × weight (kg) × time (hours)

MET (Metabolic Equivalent of Task) measures how much energy an activity uses compared to sitting at rest (1 MET). Running has a MET of ~9.8, while yoga is ~2.5. Your current body weight is factored in — heavier individuals burn more calories for the same activity.

⚠️ Estimates are averages. Actual calorie burn varies with fitness level, age, and effort.
